# IPI-Based VAT Modulation Model
## A Framework for Sovereign Tax Authorities

### 1. The "Fiscal Factor" Logic
The IPI Protocol provides a standardized value where **100 is the Neutral Reference**. Governments apply a fiscal multiplier based on this score.

### 2. Implementation Example

| IPI Score | Performance | Suggested VAT Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **< 70** | Exceptional | **Super-Reduced Rate (e.g. 0-5%)** |
| **70 - 95** | High | **Reduced Rate (e.g. 10%)** |
| **95 - 105**| Standard | **Standard Rate (e.g. 20%)** |
| **> 105** | Poor | **Surcharge Rate (e.g. 25%+)** |

### 3. Revenue Neutrality (The Bonus-Malus)
To avoid budgetary gaps, revenue services can adapt their captation by defining the category thresholds on IPI Score and the suggested VAT treatments. Ideally, the revenue generated by the "Pollution Surcharge" on high-IPI products directly finances the "Sustainable Rebate" on low-IPI essentials. This ensures social justice without increasing the overall tax burden.

### 4. Anti-Fraud Mechanism
The link between the **Digital Product Passport (DPP)** and the **VAT return** ensures that tax levels are applied based on verified, immutable blockchain data.
